# Krishi Saathi – Requirements Document

## 1. Project Overview
Krishi Saathi is an AI-powered, outcome-driven digital assistant designed to help Indian farmers access government schemes, market information, and application guidance in a simple and trusted manner.  
The system prioritizes voice-first interaction, explainable decision-making, and real-world outcome tracking.

---

## 2. Problem Statement
Many farmers are unable to benefit from government schemes due to:
- Lack of clear and reliable information
- Difficulty understanding eligibility criteria
- Language and literacy barriers
- No guidance after applying to schemes

Existing solutions mostly provide information but do not track whether farmers successfully receive benefits.

---

## 3. Objectives
- Provide easy access to scheme and market information
- Explain eligibility decisions in simple language
- Track outcomes after guidance is given
- Identify common reasons for application failures
- Support regional languages and low-connectivity environments

---

## 4. Target Users
- Small and marginal farmers
- Farmers in rural and semi-rural areas
- Users with limited digital literacy
- Users with limited or unstable internet access

---

## 5. Functional Requirements

### FR-1: Voice-Based Interaction
The system shall allow users to ask queries using voice input.

### FR-2: Multi-Language Support
The system shall support multiple regional Indian languages.

### FR-3: Eligibility Decision Support
The system shall check eligibility for government schemes based on user-provided inputs.

### FR-4: Explainable Decisions
The system shall clearly explain why a user is eligible or not eligible for a scheme.

### FR-5: Outcome Tracking
The system shall track whether a user applied, was approved, or was rejected after receiving guidance.

### FR-6: Failure Reason Identification
The system shall identify and record reasons for rejection or failure.

### FR-7: Trust Indicators
The system shall display official sources and confidence levels for decisions.

---

## 6. Non-Functional Requirements

### NFR-1: Usability
The system shall be easy to use for low-literacy users.

### NFR-2: Accessibility
The system shall support voice-first interaction and simple UI design.

### NFR-3: Performance
The system shall respond to user queries within an acceptable time.

### NFR-4: Scalability
The system shall be scalable to support more users and regions.

### NFR-5: Security & Privacy
The system shall not collect unnecessary personal data and shall protect user information.

---

## 7. Constraints and Assumptions
- Initial deployment may be limited to selected schemes
- Internet connectivity may be limited in rural areas
- Outcome tracking may depend on user feedback
